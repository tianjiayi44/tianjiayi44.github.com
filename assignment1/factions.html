Factions

At Kivotos there are many factions and in Blue Archive they are call ‘schools’. Different school has different style of uniforms that base on the school’s student or objectives. Currently there are 9 main school that has been stated in the game and they have their respective logo.

Main School

Abydos High School

It was named after one og the oldest cities in Ancient Egypt. Based on Egytian mythology and their students are based on specific Egyptian gods.

Gehenna Academy

It was named after Gehenna which is Jewish hell. Consists of students with demon like features like wings, horns and etc. Their students are also based on the 72 demons from the Lesser Key of Solomon.

Millennium Science School

It was named after the Millennium Problems, seven unsolved problems in Mathematics. The other reference is Millenarism belief system, which posits the idea of a ‘great transformation’ in modern society. The students here are based on mathematical equations, theorems and formulae.

Trinity General School

It was named after the Holy Trinity. Consists of students that based on angel, important names and locations from the bible.

Hyakkiyako Alliance Academy

It was named after the Hyakki Yagyo, the Parade of a Hundred Demons in Japan. The students there are based on Japanese Yokai.

Shanhaijing Senior Secondary School

It was named after the Classic of Mountains and Seas, a Chinese classic text that catalogues mythical locations and beings. The students there are based on Chinese deities and deitied kings.

Red Winter Federal Academy

It was based om Soviet Union and although it have nothing to do with religion initially, the students are based on figures from Slavic Mythology.

Valkyrie Police Academy

It was based on Norse Mythology and its students are named after the Valkyries, female divine beings that carry the sprites of warriors slain in the battle to the hall of the worthy, Valhalla.

Arius Branch School

The school is named after a Christian presbyter named Arius and the heretical doctrine that he pushed, which went against the doctrine of Trinitarianism.
